About Me

I'm Elizabeth Sowunmi and a Computer Scientist

I am part of the generation who grew up seeing technology develop into what it has became to today. Facial recognition, 3d printing and artificial intelligence are all concepts that would have been seen as foreign a few decades ago. Reflecting on the development of technologies as years have gone by has been nothing but satisfying to watch and I feel I have been indirectly part of. My first ever phone was a pink Blackberry Curve which only had one camera at the back with a 320p video resolution. Compared to now where having a phone with one camera is completely unheard of and most phones have HD resolution or 4K resolution. I love computers. I have always loved computers. I am always the one in my family to be called to fix a technological issue, small or large. I have always been known to be equipped with my tech skills. During my secondary school and sixth form period of my life, I did not do any technology/IT based subjects due to the lack of options and technology not being my interest at the time. My interest sparked up again when we bought our first iMac desktop and I spent 6 hours straight using it, playing around with the features and admiring the design of it. After this, I recognised that I clearly have an interest in technology and I was eager to take my interest to a degree level. I ended up receiving 5/5 offers for Computer Science in all the universities I applied to but I chose Leicester due to the excellent research carried out by the department, the level of teaching and the content we would be learning. My journey into Computer Science has not been an easy path, I have had adversities through my journey but I am proud of where I am today and where I will be in the years to come.
